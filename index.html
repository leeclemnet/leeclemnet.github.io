Hi there! My name is Lee and I'm a Ph.D. Candidate in the Space and Terrestrial Autonomous Robotic Systems (STARS) Lab at the University of Toronto Institute for Aerospace Studies (UTIAS). Originally from Winnipeg, Manitoba (affectionately: Winterpeg, Manisnowba), I hold two Bachelor's degrees from the University of Manitoba, one in Commerce (B.Comm.) and the other in Physics and Computer Science (B.Sc.).

My research lies at the interface of computer vision and machine learning, with a view to developing robust visual navigation algorithms for autonomous vehicles. Together with my advisor, Professor Jonathan Kelly, I am currently investigating how we can use machine learning to reason over more than just the geometry of an environment and improve visual navigation for autonomous robotic vehicles.

Apart from research, I am currently a Teaching Assistant for ROB 301 (Introduction to Robotics) and will be a Course Instructor for AER 521 (Mobile Robotics and Perception) in the Winter 2018 term. I am also the (co-)President of the UTIAS Aerospace Students Association. I previously served on the Board of Directors for Students of the Exploration and Development of Space – Canada (SEDS-Canada), a national student-run not-for-profit organization dedicated to improving the Canadian space sector. In my spare time I can often be found soaking up Toronto's vibrant music scene and binge-watching Netflix.
